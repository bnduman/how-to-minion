# Creating a Page

This guide shows you how to create a new page from scratch in Plone.

---

## Before You Start

Make sure you're in the right section of the website. Check the breadcrumb trail at the top of the page. If you want to add a page under "Research → Projects," navigate there first.

> 💡 New pages are created inside whatever folder you're currently in. If you create a page in the wrong place, don't worry. You can move it later (see [Organizing Content](organizing-content.md)).

---

## Step 1: Click "Add new..."

1. In the green toolbar, click **Add new...**
2. A dropdown menu will appear with several content types.
3. Click **Page**.

<!-- Screenshot suggestion: the "Add new..." dropdown with Page highlighted -->

---

## Step 2: Add a Title

1. You'll see a form with a **Title** field at the top.
2. Type a clear, descriptive title. This will appear as the page heading and in the site's navigation.

**Good example:** "Call for Papers: Summer School 2026"
**Bad example:** "New Page" or "Document1"

---

## Step 3: Write Your Content

Below the title, you'll see the **text editor**. This is where you write the body of your page. It works similarly to a basic word processor.

You can:

- Type and format text (bold, italic, headings).
- Add links.
- Insert images.

Each of these is covered in its own guide. For now, just write your text.

> 💡 Don't worry about getting everything perfect on the first try. You can always come back and edit.

---

## Step 4: Add a Summary (Optional but Recommended)

Scroll down to find the **Summary** or **Description** field. This is a short sentence or two that describes what the page is about. It appears in search results and page listings.

**Example:** "Information and deadlines for the 2026 BCDSS Summer School application."

---

## Step 5: Save Your Page

1. Scroll to the bottom of the form.
2. Click **Save**.

Your page is now created, but it is **not yet visible to the public**. It's saved as a draft. To make it live, you'll need to go through the publishing workflow (see [Publishing Workflow](publishing-workflow.md)).

---

## Editing an Existing Page

If you need to make changes to a page that already exists:

1. Navigate to the page you want to edit.
2. Click **Edit** in the green toolbar.
3. Make your changes.
4. Click **Save**.

---

## Quick Tips

- Always give your page a **clear, descriptive title**.
- Fill in the **Summary** field. It takes 10 seconds and makes your content easier to find.
- Remember to **save** before navigating away. Unsaved changes will be lost.
- Don't forget: saving is not the same as publishing. Your page is still a draft until you submit it for review.
