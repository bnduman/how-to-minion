# Tiles

Each tile does something specific: one displays text, another shows an image, another embeds a video, and so on.

Think of tiles like LEGO bricks. Each one has a shape and a purpose, and you combine them to build your page.

---

## How to Add a Tile

1. Go to **Edit**.
2. Click on **Tile**
3. A menu will appear with all available tile types.
4. Click and drag the one you want.
5. Configure the tile's settings by moving your cursor on it and finding the blue gear icon.
6. Click **Save** when you're done.

> 💡 You can rearrange tiles by dragging by the blue gear icon.

## Tile Reference

### Frame

Adds a visual frame or container around a section of content. Use this to visually group related elements together or to make a section stand out from the rest of the page.

---

### Audio

Embeds an audio file on the page. Use this for podcasts, recorded lectures, or interview clips. Upload the audio file and the tile will display a player that visitors can use to listen directly on the page.

---

### Button

Adds a clickable button that links to another page, a file, or an external website. Use this when you want a call-to-action to stand out (e.g. "Register Now" or "Download Programme"). Buttons are more visible than regular text links, and we can make them pretty.

---

### Accordion

Creates a collapsible section: visitors see a heading, and when they click it, the content expands underneath. Use this for FAQs, long lists of information, or any content where you want to keep the page clean and let visitors choose what to read.

---

### Collection

Displays a dynamic list of content from elsewhere on the site, filtered by criteria like tags, content type, or location. For example, a Collection tile can automatically show "all News items tagged with 'Events'." The list updates itself as new content is added.

> 💡 This is one of the more powerful tiles. It is worth playing around with it first before deploying.

---

### Download

Adds a download link for a file (PDF, Word document, spreadsheet, etc.). Visitors see a clear download button or link. Basically a preset button, really.

---

### Icon

Displays a small icon on the page. Useful for visual accents, section markers, or navigation elements. You can usually choose from a library of available icons.

---

### Image

Displays a single image on the page. You can upload a new image or select one that's already in Plone. Remember to fill in the copyright information (missing copyright info will stop it from displaying) and, as good practice, add alt text.

---

### Line

Adds a horizontal line (divider) across the page. Use this to visually separate sections of content. Simple but useful for breaking up long pages.

---

### News

Displays a list of recent news items from the site. The tile pulls from the News section automatically, so you don't need to update it manually. New articles appear as they're published. Refer to [news-events.md](news-events.md).

---

### Slider

Creates an image slideshow that visitors can click or swipe through. Use this for photo galleries, event highlights, or showcasing multiple images without taking up too much vertical space. Make sure all images have copyright information filled in.

---

### Spacer-Tile

Adds empty vertical space between other tiles. Use this to create breathing room between sections. Don't overdo it, but a little spacing can make a page much easier to read. You can extend ih horizontally as well as vertically.

---

### Headlines

Adds a heading to the page. Use this to introduce a new section. Choose the appropriate heading level (Heading 2 for main sections, Heading 3 for subsections). Avoid using Heading 1, as that's reserved for the page title.

---

### Text

The most common tile. Adds a block of formatted text with a built-in editor, similar to a simple word processor. You can write paragraphs, add bold/italic formatting, insert links, and create lists. Most of your content will live in Text tiles.

---

### Timeline

Displays events or milestones in a visual timeline format. Use this for project histories, chronological overviews, or "how we got here" sections. Each entry on the timeline typically has a date and a short description. I have only used this once- in World of Roman Bonn page.

---

### Video

Embeds a video on the page. You can usually paste a link from YouTube, Vimeo, or upload a video file directly. Use this for recorded lectures, event recaps, or promotional videos. Try not to add too many videos to a single page- it can considerably increase the loading times.

---

### Map

Embeds an interactive map on the page. Use this to show a location (e.g. the venue for an event, or the BCDSS building). You'll typically enter an address or coordinates to set the map's centre point.

---

### iFrame

Embeds external content from another website inside your page. The content appears as if it's part of your page, but it's actually loaded from elsewhere. Use this for embedding forms, surveys, external tools, or interactive content. See [Using iFrames](iFrame.md).


---

### RSS

Displays a live feed of content from an external website's RSS feed. The content updates automatically. Use this to show news or blog posts from partner organisations or related projects. See [Using RSS](RSS.md).

---

### Person Businesscard

Displays a person's contact information in a formatted card layout: name, title, photo, email, phone number, and links. Use this for staff pages, team directories, or anywhere you introduce a BCDSS-affiliated person. See [Creating Business Cards](business-cards.md).

---

### Bonnus Search Entry

Embeds a search bar connected to the University of Bonn's Bonnus catalogue (the university's library search system). Visitors can search the catalogue directly from your page without leaving the BCDSS website.

---

### Teaser

Creates a preview card that links to another page or piece of content. It typically shows a thumbnail image, a title, and a short summary. Use this to highlight or promote specific content (e.g. "Featured Project" or "Upcoming Event") and encourage visitors to click through.

---

## Quick Tips

- **Start with the basics.** Most pages only need a combination of Text, Image, and Headlines tiles.
- **Don't use too many tile types on one page.** Keep it simple. A page with 10 different tile types can feel cluttered.
- **Use Spacer and Line tiles sparingly** to create visual structure without overdoing it.
- **Always preview your page** before publishing to see how the tiles look together.
- **When in doubt, use a Text tile.** It's the most flexible and familiar option.
