# Plone Etiquette

A few ground rules to keep our website consistent, professional, and easy to maintain.

---

## The Basics

### Links always open in a new tab
Every link you add — whether it's to an external website, a PDF, or another page — should be set to open in a new tab. This way, visitors never lose their place on our site.

### Always link our people
Whenever you mention a fellow, alumnus, or any BCDSS-affiliated person by name, link to their profile page. This keeps our website interconnected and gives our people visibility.

### Always tag your News and Events
Before you publish, make sure you've added the correct tags. Without them, your post won't appear in the right collection, and nobody will see it.

### Get a second pair of eyes before you publish
Ask a colleague to review your page before it goes live. Fresh eyes catch typos, broken links, and formatting issues that you'll miss after staring at something for too long.

### Add alt text to images
It is not required, but good practice, to add alt text to your images. Alt text is the short description that appears when an image can't load, and it helps with accessibility. Something like *"Photo of Prof. Hillner at the 2025 conference"* is much better than leaving it blank.

### Check your links after publishing
Visit your page after it goes live and click every link. Links break more often than you'd think, especially links to external websites. A quick check takes 30 seconds and saves embarrassment.

### Credit images and always include copyright information
If you use an image, make sure you have the rights to it and note the source. This is especially important for stock photos or images from social media. Be aware that an image with missing copyright information can break the page or its content.
